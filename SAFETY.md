# Safety

Pillion puts content on the dashboard of a **moving motorcycle**. Riding is dangerous; a screen in
your field of view can make it more so. Read this before you use it.

## The short version

- **Set up your route while parked.** Never type, search, or tap your phone while moving.
- **Use a glanceable map only.** Do not mirror video, messages, social media, or anything that pulls
  your attention off the road.
- **Your eyes belong on the road**, not on the dash. Treat the mirrored map like you'd treat a
  glance at a speedometer — a fraction of a second, no more.
- **If in doubt, pull over.**

## This is experimental software

Pillion is a hobby / interoperability project, provided **as-is, with no warranty** (see
[LICENSE.md](LICENSE.md)). It is **not** a safety device, not certified, and **not affiliated with or
endorsed by Yamaha or Garmin**. It can disconnect, lag, freeze, show a stale frame, or stop without
warning. Never rely on it for anything safety-critical.

## You are responsible

- **You** are responsible for riding safely and lawfully at all times.
- Local laws differ on what may be displayed or operated while riding. Displaying a phone screen on
  your dash may be restricted or illegal where you ride. **Check your local rules** — it's on you.
- The dash is for **assistance only**. Keep full attention on traffic, road conditions, and your
  surroundings.

## Battery & device heat

Screen capture, JPEG encoding, GPS, and continuous Bluetooth drain the phone and generate heat —
especially in a sealed mount in direct sun. Use the in-app **Battery & quality** settings to lower
quality / cap the frame rate, keep the phone cool, and watch your charge on long rides.

## Reporting a safety problem

If you find behavior that could endanger a rider (e.g. a crash that locks the dash, or a way the app
can grab attention unexpectedly), please open an issue and label it clearly. Safety reports are
prioritized.
